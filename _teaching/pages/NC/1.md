This lecture introduces the foundations of numerical computing, focusing on well-posedness, sources of error, stability, conditioning, floating-point arithmetic, and computational complexity.

## Topics Covered
- Mathematical foundations: well-posed problems, sources of numerical error, error propagation
- Stability and conditioning: forward/backward error, condition numbers, matrix conditioning
- Floating point arithmetic: IEEE 754, machine epsilon, catastrophic cancellation
- Algorithm stability: forward vs backward stability, analysis techniques
- Computational complexity: Big-O and design trade-offs

## Learning Objectives
- Understand well-posed vs ill-posed problems
- Analyze error propagation and stability
- Evaluate conditioning and algorithmic robustness
